Whats with the Name?

"Mephisto" is a nod to the 19th-century chess-playing automaton of the same name.

During its time, the mechanism appeared to be able to play a strong game of chess against a human opponent. It's been said that Mephisto never lost a game. It was the first automaton to win a Chess tournament when it was entered in the Counties Chess Association in London in 1878.

However, it was later revealed that Mephisto was, in fact, nothing more than an elaborate illusion. A chess master hiding away from sight would remotely control the machine by electromechanical means, tricking unsuspecting players.

In modern times, chess engines far outclass even the best of human players. Ironically, the chess masters of today are machines.
